What is a Sedentary Lifestyle

A sedentary lifestyle is a type of lifestyle where an individual involves little or no physical activity. A person living a sedentary lifestyle is frequently sitting or lying down while engaged in an activity like reading, socializing, watching television, playing video games, or using a mobile phone/computer for most of the day. It is common to spend large amounts of time being sedentary when at work, when travelling or during leisure time.

Shehu, R., Abdullahi, A., & Adekeye, D. (2010)

     

Sedentary Lifestyle Health Effects

Prolonged sitting is associated with a range of health problems.

Cancer

Physical inactivity may increase the risks of certain cancers.

Cardiovascular diseases

Physical inactivity has been shown to be a risk factor for certain cardiovascular diseases.

Depression

Physical inactivity may contribute to anxiety and depression.

Blood Pressure & Cholesterol

Physical inactivity is linked to high blood pressure and elevated cholesterol levels.

Overweight

People who are more active are less likely to be overweight or obese.

Decrease in skeletal muscle mass

Sitting too much may cause a decrease in skeletal muscle mass.

Johns Hopkins Medicine (2019)
